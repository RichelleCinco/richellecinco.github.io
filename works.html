Packaging

I love packaging. As much as I enjoy the digital aspects of my work, there is something to be said about being able to physically touch what you're creating. Three dimensional objects is an area I find myself comfortable with.

My packaging design for Buckingham Pewter, a pewter company based in Australia, who pride themselves with quality product that have stood the test of time, started with the redesign of their logo as part of the rebranding project. My intent was to bring a beautiful old world craftsmanship into the twenty-first century while celebrating history and stories to be told for generations.

Tomi, on the other hand was a packaging idea for a fictitious company I created based on five different words provided as part of the restrictions of the project. Tomi is a toddler plant-based supplement. Its name is a portmanteau of the words “toddler” and “milk”. With the market saturated with a lot of milk products, I opted for a different type of packaging not only for the intent of standing out on the shelves, but also for functionality reasons - based on user need for something that is portable and easy for parents to use who have their hands full, especially, when they are on the go with young children.
