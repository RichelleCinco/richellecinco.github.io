The Weeks at a Glance

Weeks 1 & 2 : Understanding HTML. The first two weeks were about learning the very structure of a webpage. I like to think of it as the backbone. Understanding its syntax and organization, and how to layer them through nesting were my biggest takeaways.


Weeks 3 & 4 : CSS. These were the simple and fun weeks. Prior, I never knew or understood what CSS was about. It was like opening a beautiful box of goodies with lots of confetti, and colors to choose from. Animations was another great thing to learn.


Weeks 4 & 5 : Javascript and Bootstrap. Well, I'll start with Javascript. Opposite to how I felt with CSS, JS was a whole different language. My biggest mistake was not spending enough time with the material (even more than I did with the first four weeks). I am continuing to learn it outside of this class because I don't think I fully grasped it or retained most of it. I'll talk about Bootstrap below.


Weeks 5 & 6 : Bootstrap. Hello Bootstrap! You are the reason I am smiling after 7-weeks of trying. My site became alive, with personality, and with hope. I sometimes feel like cheating, but I'll take it any day, especially as a beginner. Learning about the grid made a whole difference with my design approach. I absorbed this material much quicker this time around than any other sections, perhaps because of its visually appealing, and easy to follow. When combined with Javascript, it's a beautiful concoction!


Weeks 7: Final Site. I would like to think the end feels like the beginning. It's a culmination of all the things I learned for the past six weeks. I am pleased with my learnings, even if, at times, I felt that my work was inadequate in some areas; however, I learned to ease up and try to enjoy putting the puzzle pieces together even if it was less than perfect.
